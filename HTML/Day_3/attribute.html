Author

Paulo Coelho (born 1947) is a renowned Brazilian author best known for his international bestseller The Alchemist. His work, translated into over 80 languages, often centers on spirituality, self-discovery, and pursuing one's "Personal Legend". A former songwriter and journalist, he is a UN Messenger of Peace and a member of the Brazilian Academy of Letters

Background: Born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, he had a tumultuous youth, including being institutionalized by his parents for refusing a traditional career.

Career Transformation: Before becoming a novelist, he was a lyricist for Brazilian rock music, a journalist, and a theater director. He worked in the music industry and was briefly involved in political activism, leading to a brief kidnapping during Brazil's dictatorship.

Literary Success: After a spiritual pilgrimage on the Road to Santiago in 1986, he committed to writing. His 1988 novel The Alchemist became a global phenomenon, selling over 100 million copies.

Themes and Style: His books often explore themes of destiny, magic, and, as in The Pilgrimage, spiritual journeys. His writing is known for being direct and simple, though he has faced criticism for lacking deep literary finesse.

Activism: He is a United Nations Messenger of Peace and works to support underprivileged members of society.

Personal Life: He lives with his wife, artist Christina Oiticica, in Geneva, Switzerland, and is an avid practitioner of archery
